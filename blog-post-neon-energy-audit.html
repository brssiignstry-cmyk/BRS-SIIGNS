1. Wattage Comparison: LED Neon vs. Traditional Glass Neon

The fundamental difference lies in how light is produced. Glass neon requires high-voltage transformers (up to 15,000V) to ionize gas. This is a concept explored in Wired's technical features. Our LED neon signs Trichy use low-voltage (12V/24V) semiconductor technology. This technology is a standard detailed in ISO efficiency standards. On average, an LED neon sign uses 80% less wattage than a glass sign of the same size. This is part of our energy savings guide. It’s the "divine" choice for your wallet.

The Wattage Gap: A 1-meter glass neon sign can consume 60-100 watts, while the same sign in LED neon consumes only 10-15 watts.
