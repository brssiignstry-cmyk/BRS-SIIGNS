1. What is Pixel Pitch? Understanding the "P" in LED Boards

Pixel pitch is the distance (in millimeters) between the center of one LED pixel and the center of the next. This is a psychological shortcut discussed in Wired's features on visual attention. A smaller pixel pitch means higher pixel density and higher resolution. For an LED Running Display Board Trichy, this determines how "smooth" the text looks. This aligns with MarketingProfs' visual impact studies. This is part of our ultimate guide to branding. It turns your shopfront into a "divine" and crisp attraction.

The Pitch Rule: The smaller the number after the "P," the higher the resolution and the closer the viewing distance can be without seeing individual dots.

The "P" stands for Pitch, which is the distance in millimeters between the centers of two adjacent pixels.

P6 offers higher resolution, but P10 is often more cost-effective for signs that are viewed from a long distance.

A simple rule is that the pixel pitch in millimeters equals the minimum viewing distance in meters.

Yes, because there are more LEDs per square meter, a smaller pitch board typically consumes more power.
