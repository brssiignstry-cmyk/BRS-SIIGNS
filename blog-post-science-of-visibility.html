12. 15-Point Visibility FAQ Masterclass

1. What is a "Nit" in LED signage? +
A Nit is a unit of measurement for brightness. Outdoor signs typically need 5,000+ Nits to be visible in direct sunlight.
2. Why does my sign look blurry at night? +
This is usually caused by "glare" or "haloing" because the sign is too bright for the dark environment. Adjustable drivers can fix this.
3. What is the best viewing angle for a corner shop? +
A wide viewing angle of 140 degrees is ideal for corner shops to capture traffic from both intersecting roads.
4. Can I use "Script" fonts for my outdoor sign? +
We don't recommend it for primary branding as script fonts are difficult to read from a distance. Sans-serif is much better.
5. How does humidity affect visibility? +
Humidity can scatter light, creating a "haze" around the letters. High-index acrylic faces help minimize this effect.
6. What is the best color for high visibility? +
White and Yellow/Gold provide the highest contrast against dark backgrounds, making them the most visible colors.
7. How big should my letters be for a highway? +
For highway speeds, we recommend at least 15-20 inch tall letters to give drivers enough time to read your name.
8. Do you provide a "Visibility Audit"? +
Yes, BRS SIIGNS provides free on-site visibility audits to calculate the perfect size and brightness for your location.
9. What is "Cool White" vs "Warm White"? +
Cool White (6000K) is bluish and high-visibility; Warm White (3000K) is yellowish and creates a premium, cozy feel.
10. Can I change the brightness of my sign? +
Yes, our premium LED sign boards come with adjustable drivers that can be tuned for day and night visibility.
11. Why is contrast more important than size? +
A small sign with high contrast is often more readable than a large sign with low contrast. Contrast defines the letters.
12. How often should I clean my sign? +
In a dusty city like Trichy, we recommend a light cleaning every 3-6 months to maintain maximum brightness.
